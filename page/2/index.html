Jessie FausetGetty Images1882-1961Jessie Fauset graduated from Cornell University in 1905 and began working as a teacher. During this time, her essays, poems, and short stories drew the attention of W.E.B. Du Bois, who hired her as the literary editor of the NAACP’s official magazine The Crisis in 1919. In the role, Fauset helped elevate the works of other prominent Harlem writers like Jean Toomer and Claude McKay. Fauset also wrote four novels of her own and served as co-editor for The Brownies’ Book, a publication that taught African American children about their heritage.
