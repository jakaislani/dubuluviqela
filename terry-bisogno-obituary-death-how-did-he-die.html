    The Voice Of Long Island: Terry Bisogno Obituary

    On August 5, 2023, North Massapequa, New York, bid farewell to Terry Bisogno, a revered race announcer who bravely fought colon cancer.

    Terry’s obituary underscores his significant role in the DCRP (Long Island’s esteemed running group), where he fostered strong camaraderie among its members.

    He empowered each member, nurturing a genuine running team spirit and inspiring them to reach their personal bests and celebrate shared victories.

    Terry’s uplifting finish-line cheers became synonymous with DCRP races, leaving an indelible mark on those fortunate to witness them.

    Terry’s impact on the Long Island running community was truly exceptional. Bisogno’s contagious enthusiasm, warm-heartedness, and unwavering support touched countless lives.

    He extended his generous spirit by mentoring fledgling runners, orchestrating charitable events, and actively engaging in diverse community projects.

    This sincere commitment created an enduring and uplifting legacy that reached far beyond the boundaries of the running world.

    Terry Bisogno’s family will later communicate details about his obituary and funeral arrangements.

    Terry Bisogno Death: How Did The Race Announcer Die?

    As mentioned earlier, Terry Bisogno died due to colon cancer, a battle he was facing for quite a time and was previously admitted to hospital on the first week of July.

    Known as the “Voice of Long Island,” Terry called more than 50 races a year, even while battling colon cancer.

    During his impressive career, he accomplished extraordinary accomplishments and established multiple records, motivating budding athletes and runners globally.

    Whether conquering demanding ultra-marathons or guiding his team to triumph in esteemed races, Terry’s unwavering enthusiasm for running was unmatched.

    In July, a walk and run event took place in his hometown of Massapequa, with hundreds of runners participating in front of his house.

    “People had been trying to organize something for him, but he resisted,” said Sami Saad from Landshark Endurance. “We kept it a secret to surprise him.”

    Other runners also expressed their deep appreciation for the beloved announcer.

    Terry was in the hospital then, so organizers arranged for him to virtually witness the event through Zoom and connect with the running community.

    Moreover, a GoFundMe campaign, organized by Rich Sallustro, was launched to support Terry.

    The campaign highlighted that while medical insurance covered a significant portion of treatment costs, there were still numerous expensive co-pays for various aspects of cancer treatment.

    These often overlooked expenses, such as special diets, transportation, and other unexpected costs, added to the financial burden.

    Unfortunately, despite the efforts, Terry ultimately lost his battle with cancer.
